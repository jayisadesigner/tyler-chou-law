"Let's just refund him."

I said to my team.

This week, I returned legal fees to a client after a full year of fighting to restore his 7 deleted YouTube channels. I had to admit defeat.

Here's what happened:

My client violated copyright on ONE channel. His smallest one. But because all seven channels shared the same email address, YouTube deleted everything.

10 years of his life's work. Gone.

After a year of working with YouTube executives, here's what I learned:

YouTube's position has changed. They used to help on a case-by-case basis. Not anymore. They no longer step in on copyright strikes or channel deletions.

This isn't rare. I get 3-5 requests weekly from creators with deleted channels.

The brutal truth? Once your channel is deleted, I'm not as effective.

But if you come with 1-2 copyright strikes? Community violations? Demonetization? I can usually reverse those.

## Here's What's Keeping Me Up at Night

Most creators don't realize they're one strike away from losing everything. No backup plan. No email list. No direct access to their audience.

That's why I've become obsessed with two things:

### 1. Clean IP from Day One

Use your own b-roll. License your music properly. Build a clean chain of title. This isn't just about avoiding strikes - it's about being ready for an exit when opportunity knocks. IP trolls are multiplying exponentially.

### 2. Building on Your Own Land

Your email list. Your community. Your direct relationships. The things no algorithm can take away.

I tell every creator: You need at least 5 revenue streams. Up to 15 if you want a real exit.

But the most important one? A community built on YOUR land.

## Walking My Talk

So I launched The Creators' Attorney Collective last week on Kajabi. Not just because I want direct access to my people, but because I need to walk my talk.
